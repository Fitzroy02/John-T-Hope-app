# HOPENISM IP POLICY

## Intellectual Property Framework for Hopenism CIC

This document outlines Hopenism's approach to intellectual property management, licensing, and usage rights.

---

## 1. Platform Purpose

**Hopenism** is a Community Interest Company (CIC) operating a digital platform for:

- Mental health and wellbeing support
- Educational content delivery
- Community engagement
- Symbolic teaching tools
- Artist and creator partnerships

Hopenism licenses content from external creators to fulfill its public benefit mission.

---

## 2. IP Ownership Model

### 2.1 Hopenism-Owned IP
Hopenism owns:

- Platform code and architecture
- Original educational modules
- User interface designs
- Hopenism branding and logo
- Marketing materials
- Data collection frameworks

### 2.2 Licensed IP
Hopenism licenses from external creators:

- Fictional characters (e.g., Mr Walker from John T. Hope)
- Music and audio content
- Written narratives
- Educational materials
- Artistic contributions

**All licensed IP remains the property of the original creator.**

### 2.3 User-Generated Content
User contributions (comments, feedback, forum posts):

- Remain the property of users
- Grant Hopenism a licence to display and use within the platform
- May be moderated or removed per Terms of Service
- Do not transfer ownership to Hopenism

---

## 3. Licensing Philosophy

### 3.1 Fair Compensation
Hopenism believes creators deserve fair payment for their work.

We implement:

- Performance-based royalties
- Usage-linked compensation
- Transparent reporting
- Regular payments
- Clear accounting

### 3.2 Creator Rights Protection
Hopenism respects creator rights by:

- Maintaining clear ownership records
- Requiring approval for modifications
- Protecting moral rights
- Preventing unauthorized sublicensing
- Enforcing usage boundaries

### 3.3 Mission Alignment
Hopenism licenses content that:

- Supports mental health and wellbeing
- Serves educational purposes
- Provides public benefit
- Aligns with CIC values
- Enhances user experience

---

## 4. Character Licensing: Mr Walker

### 4.1 Ownership
**Mr Walker** is exclusively owned by **John T. Hope**.

### 4.2 Usage Rights
Hopenism has a **non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable licence** to use Mr Walker for:

- Symbolic teaching purposes
- Educational modules
- Guided animations
- Narrative sequences
- User engagement

### 4.3 Restrictions
Hopenism may NOT:

- Claim ownership of Mr Walker
- Create unauthorized derivative works
- Modify character identity without approval
- Sublicense to third parties
- Use in commercial endorsements

### 4.4 Compensation
Hopenism pays:

1. **Discounted annual appearance fee**
2. **Performance-based royalty** per user interaction

Full details in `/legal/LICENCE_MR_WALKER_DUAL_STREAM.md`

---

## 5. Music and Artist Licensing

### 5.1 Artist Partnerships
Hopenism partners with independent musicians to provide:

- Therapeutic soundscapes
- Meditation music
- Ambient content
- Educational audio

### 5.2 Compensation Model
Artists receive:

- **Streaming-based royalties** (£0.003–£0.01 per play, or negotiated rate)
- Transparent monthly reporting
- Direct payment from Hopenism
- Attribution and credit

### 5.3 Rights Retained
Artists retain:

- Full ownership of compositions
- Right to use music elsewhere
- Commercial exploitation rights
- Moral rights

---

## 6. Educational Content Licensing

### 6.1 External Educators
Hopenism may license educational content from:

- Mental health professionals
- Wellness experts
- Academic researchers
- Educational content creators

### 6.2 Terms
Educators receive:

- Fair compensation for content
- Attribution and credit
- Usage reporting
- Licensing agreements

Content may be:

- Modules or courses
- Written materials
- Video or audio content
- Interactive tools

---

## 7. Brand Separation and Independence

### 7.1 Hopenism as Licensee
Hopenism operates as a **licensee**, not an IP owner, for external content.

This separation ensures:

- Clear legal boundaries
- Protected creator rights
- Independent brand identities
- Scalable partnerships

### 7.2 Creator Independence
Licensed creators (like John T. Hope) retain:

- Full creative control
- Right to develop IP elsewhere
- Media adaptation rights
- Commercial freedom

Hopenism's licence does not restrict external development.

---

## 8. Approval and Modification Process

### 8.1 Content Approval
All use of licensed IP requires:

1. **Initial approval** from IP owner
2. **Review of implementation** by Hopenism governance
3. **Final sign-off** before release

### 8.2 Modifications
Any changes to licensed content require:

- Written approval from IP owner
- Documentation of changes
- Updated licensing terms (if necessary)

### 8.3 Workflow
See `/governance/IP_APPROVAL_WORKFLOW.md` for detailed process.

---

## 9. Accounting and Reporting

### 9.1 Internal Ledger Structure
Hopenism maintains separate accounting lines for each licensed IP:

**Example for Mr Walker:**
- `MRW_APPEARANCE_FEE`
- `MRW_ROYALTY_STREAM`

**Example for Music Artists:**
- `ARTIST_[NAME]_ROYALTY`

### 9.2 Reporting Frequency
- **Monthly:** Interaction data collection
- **Quarterly:** Royalty calculations and payments
- **Annual:** Appearance fees and contract renewals

### 9.3 Transparency
All creators receive:

- Detailed usage reports
- Interaction analytics
- Payment breakdowns
- Audit trails

---

## 10. User Terms and Fair Use

### 10.1 User Rights
Hopenism users may:

- Access licensed content within the platform
- Engage with characters and modules
- Share experiences and feedback
- Recommend content to others

### 10.2 User Restrictions
Users may NOT:

- Download or redistribute licensed content
- Claim ownership of IP
- Create derivative works
- Use content commercially outside the platform

### 10.3 Fair Use
Standard fair use provisions apply for:

- Educational commentary
- Personal discussion
- Review and critique

---

## 11. Infringement and Enforcement

### 11.1 Hopenism's Commitment
Hopenism will:

- Protect licensed IP from infringement
- Report unauthorized use to IP owners
- Cooperate in enforcement actions
- Remove infringing content immediately

### 11.2 Creator Rights
IP owners retain full enforcement rights and may:

- Issue takedown notices
- Pursue legal action
- Revoke licences for breach
- Protect their work independently
