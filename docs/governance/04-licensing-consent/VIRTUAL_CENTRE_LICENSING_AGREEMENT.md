# Virtual Centre Licensing Agreement

**A civic covenant between the John T Hope Centre, learners, families, and institutional partners.**

---

## Purpose

This licensing agreement exists to:

- **Protect learners** — Especially children and vulnerable individuals
- **Define consent boundaries** — Clear, transparent, and revocable
- **Ensure neutrality** — Independence from political and commercial pressure
- **Ensure safeguarding compliance** — Protection as foundational priority
- **Establish responsibilities** — Mutual accountability between Centre and users
- **Maintain institutional integrity** — Long-term trust and public service
- **Prevent misuse** — Protection against misrepresentation, exploitation, or political capture

This is not a commercial contract.  
This is a **civic covenant** — a promise of mutual responsibility.

---

## Licence Principles

All licensing agreements with the John T Hope Centre are governed by:

### Plain Language
- Written in clear, accessible language
- Understandable to all reading levels
- No legalistic jargon or opacity
- Explanatory notes where complexity is unavoidable

### Transparency of Rights and Responsibilities
- Clear rights granted to users
- Clear responsibilities of users
- Clear responsibilities of the institution
- No hidden clauses or fine print

### No Commercial Incentives
- No profit motive in any agreement
- No sale of user data
- No advertising or sponsorship arrangements
- No hidden fees or commercial obligations

### Revocability
- Consent can be withdrawn at any time
- Withdrawal does not require justification
- Clear processes for ending engagement
- No penalties for withdrawal

---

## Rights Granted to Users

All users of the John T Hope Centre are granted:

### 1. Access to Age-Appropriate Materials
- Content appropriate to developmental stage
- Tier-based access controls
- Transparent content standards
- No exposure to inappropriate material

### 2. Safeguarding Protections
- Protection from harm and exploitation
- Clear reporting mechanisms
- Institutional responsibility for safety
- Ongoing safeguarding oversight

### 3. Transparent Authorship and Versioning
- Clear attribution of all content
- Documented version history
- No hidden editorial changes
- Accountability for institutional decisions

### 4. Right to Withdraw Consent
- Revocation at any time without justification
- Clear process for withdrawal
- Prompt institutional response
- No retention of data beyond legal requirements

### 5. Right to Privacy
- Minimal data collection
- Ethical data processing
- No commercial exploitation of personal information
- Clear privacy protections

### 6. Right to Institutional Neutrality
- Protection from political influence
- Protection from ideological indoctrination
- Balanced, age-appropriate content
- Transparent governance

---

## Responsibilities of Users

Users accessing the John T Hope Centre agree to:

### 1. Provide Accurate Consent Information
- Truthful identity verification
- Accurate age information
- Honest representation of role (parent/guardian/learner)
- Prompt updates to consent status if circumstances change

### 2. Respect Age-Tier Boundaries
- No access to content beyond assigned tier
- No circumvention of age controls
- No sharing of inappropriate content with younger learners
- Respect for developmental safeguards

### 3. Avoid Misuse and Misrepresentation
- No misuse of Centre resources
- No misrepresentation of Centre positions
- No unauthorized commercial use of Centre materials
- No exploitation of institutional trust

### 4. Report Safeguarding Concerns
- Prompt reporting of safeguarding issues
- Cooperation with safeguarding investigations
- Honesty in reporting
- Protection of vulnerable individuals

### 5. Respect Institutional Neutrality
- No attempts to introduce political bias
- No pressure for ideological alignment
- No misrepresentation of the Centre's purpose
- Respect for the 10-year political buffer rule

---

## Institutional Responsibilities

The John T Hope Centre commits to:

### 1. Uphold Safeguarding Standards
- Maintain robust safeguarding systems
- Respond promptly to safeguarding concerns
- Prioritize protection of children and vulnerable individuals
- Continuous improvement of safeguarding practices

### 2. Uphold Neutrality Standards
- Independence from political influence
- No ideological bias in curriculum
- Balanced, age-appropriate content
- Transparent governance free from external pressure

### 3. Maintain Transparent Authorship
- Clear attribution of all content
- Documented version history
- Public documentation of institutional decisions
- Accountability for governance changes

### 4. Protect User Data Ethically
- Minimal data collection
- Ethical data processing
- No commercial exploitation
- Secure storage and prompt deletion when consent is withdrawn

### 5. Maintain Clear Consent Mechanisms
- Simple, understandable consent processes
- Easy withdrawal of consent
- Clear documentation of consent status
- Respect for user autonomy

### 6. Maintain Clear Withdrawal Mechanisms
- Simple process for ending engagement
- Prompt response to withdrawal requests
- No penalties or pressure to remain
- Respectful disengagement

### 7. Maintain the 10-Year Political Buffer
- No curriculum influenced by political events less than 10 years old
- Protection from political capture
- Long-term institutional stability
- Generational perspective

---

## Prohibited Uses

The following uses of the John T Hope Centre are strictly prohibited:

### 1. Commercial Exploitation
- Sale or monetization of user data
- Unauthorized commercial use of materials
- Advertising or sponsorship arrangements
- Profit-driven decision-making

### 2. Political Misrepresentation
- Misrepresentation of the Centre's political stance
- Use of Centre materials for political campaigning
- Attempts to capture the Centre for political purposes
- Violation of neutrality standards

### 3. Safeguarding Violations
- Exposure of learners to inappropriate content
- Circumvention of age-tier controls
- Failure to report safeguarding concerns
- Any action that endangers children or vulnerable individuals

### 4. Misuse of Institutional Trust
- Misrepresentation of Centre positions
- Unauthorized representation of the Centre
- Exploitation of Centre reputation
- Deception regarding Centre affiliation

## Amendments and Versioning

This licensing agreement is a **living document**.

### Amendment Process
- Written justification for changes
- Clear authorship and dating
- Review by relevant governing bodies
- Public documentation of changes

### Version Control
- All versions are publicly accessible
- Clear change logs
- No retroactive changes without notification
- Transparency in institutional evolution

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this licensing agreement may result in:

- Suspension of access
- Revocation of privileges
- Reporting to relevant authorities (in cases of safeguarding violations)
- Legal action (in cases of misrepresentation or exploitation)

Enforcement decisions are made transparently, with clear reasoning and accountability.
